Environmental Education & Awareness

Understanding air quality, temperature, humidity, and their effects on health. Empowering Zambian communities with knowledge.

Temperature-Humidity Index (THI)

THI above 75 = Discomfort. Above 80 = Severe risk. Monitor to avoid heat exhaustion.

Health Effects of Poor Air Quality

High heat + humidity increases respiratory issues, dehydration, and cardiovascular strain. Children and elderly most vulnerable.

Protective Actions

Drink water, use fans, limit outdoor work during peak sun (11am-3pm), wear light clothes, use masks on high pollution days.

Zambia's Climate Challenge

Rapid urbanization creates heat islands. Green spaces and smart monitoring reduce risks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the DHT22 sensor?

The DHT22 provides ±0.5°C accuracy for temperature and ±2% for humidity, reliable for environmental monitoring.

How can I contribute data?

Use the "Add Reading" page to submit your temperature, humidity, and smoke readings. You can also contact us to integrate your own ESP32 device.

What does THI mean?

Temperature-Humidity Index combines temperature and humidity to measure thermal comfort. Above 75 indicates discomfort, above 80 is dangerous.

How can schools and clinics use this?

The dashboard is completely free and works on any device with internet access. Perfect for public awareness displays.

Is this available across all provinces?

Yes! Anyone from any Zambian province can access the dashboard and contribute data from their location.

What is the MQ-135 sensor?

The MQ-135 detects smoke, CO2, and other harmful gases. It provides relative measurements to alert when air quality deteriorates.
